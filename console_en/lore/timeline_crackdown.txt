TITLE: Condensed Timeline of the Crackdown

2086:
Signal repression intensifies after pirate broadcasts spread strike routes.
Authorities learn a simple lesson: when workers still know the paths, blockades last longer.
This marks the beginning of serious investment in targeted jamming, asymmetric prioritization, and surveillance of community channels.

2088:
Debt-enforcement APIs are integrated into district policing.
This moment matters because it destroys an old symbolic boundary: private collection no longer sits beside public order, it becomes one of its active layers.
Late payments begin producing faster physical effects: checks, denials, monitoring, and movement restrictions.

2090:
Raid wave against free-software collectives, known as the LV77 period.
The seizures target more than any single tool. They are meant to break the very idea of technical commons.
Archives ripped out, relays purged, maintainers charged, documentation scattered.
Officially it is stabilization. In practice it is an organized impoverishment of popular capacity to understand the systems governing it.

2092:
Administrative sabotage leaks expose automated eviction pipelines, IR31 period.
Logs, scripts, and prioritization tables reveal that supposedly exceptional displacements were already running like near-industrial infrastructure.
The scandal does not stop the machine, but it shatters a useful illusion: from this point on, everyone knows the systemic error was never an error.

2094:
Independent toolchains reappear through DX90-aligned mirrors.
The return is not clean triumph. The tools are fragmentary, trust chains damaged, human networks exhausted.
But something restarts: the ability to rebuild, audit, transmit, and sabotage without asking permission from dominant infrastructure.

2095:
The ZE08 blackout marks the shift from selective suppression to district-wide punishment.
It is no longer only this person, this building, this cell being targeted.
Entire neighborhoods become disciplinary demonstration zones.
The message is transparent to anyone who can read it: if an area insists on resisting, resistance will be charged back through daily life.

2096:
Sentinel autonomy patches are deployed citywide under emergency procurement.
Public discourse invokes threat, urgency, operational necessity.
In practice, the city accepts that a larger share of coercive decision-making will be moved into opaque systems that act faster and are harder to contest in real time.

Overall reading:
The crackdown is not a sequence of separate measures.
It is one coherent evolution: first monitor, then slow, then classify, then automate, then normalize.
Each stage makes the next easier to sell.
Each crisis manufactures the pretext for the following tool.
